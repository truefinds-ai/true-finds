The Problem

Amazon’s pricing is designed
to make you guess wrong.

Prices change hundreds of times a day. Sale badges get applied to inflated reference prices. You’re left guessing. Most shoppers lose.

📈

Fake markdowns

Sellers set their own reference prices. Many inflate them for weeks before a sale so the discount looks larger than it is. A “40% off” badge can mean nothing at all.

🕐

Prices that move constantly

Amazon’s algorithms adjust prices hundreds of times a day. What looked like a deal this morning may be inflated by tonight. There is no way to know without the data.

🌊

Deal fatigue

Prime Day, Lightning Deals, flash sales. Most of them are noise. Without 90 days of price history behind each badge, every deal looks urgent and nothing actually is.
